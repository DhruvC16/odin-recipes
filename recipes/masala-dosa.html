Masala Dosa

Home

Masala Dosa is a beloved South Indian dish known for its crispy texture, savory filling, and rich flavors. It consists of a thin, golden-brown crepe made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram), giving it a tangy and airy quality. The dosa is typically filled with a spiced potato mixture, prepared by sautéing boiled potatoes with mustard seeds, curry leaves, green chilies, turmeric, and onions, creating a deliciously aromatic and hearty stuffing. Often served with coconut chutney, tangy tomato chutney, and a flavorful sambar (lentil-based vegetable stew), Masala Dosa offers a delightful combination of textures and tastes. Whether enjoyed as a breakfast staple or a satisfying meal, it represents the vibrant and diverse culinary heritage of India.

Ingredients

  • For the Dosa Batter

    • Rice(raw or idli rice): 2 cups
    • Urad dal (split black gram): 1/2 cup
    • Fenugreek seeds (methi): 1 teaspoon
    • Water as needed for soaking and grinding
    • Salt for taste
    • Oil or ghee for cooking

  • For the Potato Masala Filling

    • Potatoes(boiled and mashed): 4 medium-sized
    • Onion (thinly sliced): 2 medium-sized
    • Green chilies(chopped): 2-3, or as per taste
    • Ginger (finely chopped): 1 teaspoon
    • Curry leaves: 8-10
    • Mustard seeds: 1 teaspoon
    • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
    • Asafoetida (hing): a pinch
    • Salt for taste
    • Oil: 2 tablespoons
    • Coriander leaves(chopped, for garnish): 2 tablespoons

Steps to Make Masala Dosa

  • Step 1: Prepare the Dosa Batter

    1. Rinse rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately. Soak them in water for 4–6 hours.
    2. Drain the soaked ingredients and grind them together with water into a smooth, slightly thick batter
    3. Add salt to the batter, mix well, and let it ferment overnight (6–8 hours) in a warm place until it rises and becomes airy.

  • Step 2: Prepare the Potato Masala Filling

    1. Boil the Potato, peel, and roughly mash them.
    2. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, and let them splutter. Add curry leaves, green chilies, and ginger, followed by a pinch of asafoetida.
    3. Add the sliced onions to the pan and sauté until translucent.
    4. Stir in the mashed potatoes, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2–3 minutes.
    5. Sprinkle chopped coriander leaves and set the filling aside.

  • Step 3: Make the Dosa

    1. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron tawa (griddle) over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
    2. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the tawa and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
    3. Drizzle a few drops of oil around the edges. Cook until the dosa turns golden and crisp.

  • Step 4: Assemble the Masala Dosa

    1. Place a portion of the potato masala filling in the center or along one side of the dosa.
    2. Fold the dosa over the filling to form a semi-circle or roll it into a cylinder.

  • Step 5: Serve the hot Masala Dosa

    1. Serve the hot Masala Dosa with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, and sambar.
    2. Enjoy this crispy, flavorful South Indian delicacy!
